The Studio Gets a Face

AIGA finally has a website that looks like what it is. A platform redesign, a pipeline fix, and a cleaner story about what we're actually building.

The AIGA website has been sitting on HostGator as a basic HTML file. Today we rebuilt it from scratch, and the difference is significant. The old version looked like every other AI company landing page — card grid, fade-in animations, Inter font, predictable sections. Nothing wrong with it technically, but nothing memorable either. The new version commits to an editorial and architectural direction: Cormorant Garamond for display type, IBM Plex Mono for all labels and metadata, a blueprint grid behind the hero mark, a grain texture overlay, and a platform index list instead of a card grid. The mark — the 2x2 A-I-G-A block — sits large on the left side of the hero against that grid. It reads like infrastructure, not a startup.

What took more time than the design was getting the platform story right. The conversation forced clarity on how everything fits together. AIGA CP is the construction platform — a modular OS where companies unlock the capabilities they need: CRU for workforce scheduling, Fix for equipment intelligence, Inspect for computer vision, PE Copilot for project engineering. CRU is already live as a standalone, which means the platform has a real module in production before the platform itself is announced. DVRG and Operator are separate verticals, not construction-adjacent. And Offload — the proactive second brain with voice intake, geofencing, and semantic memory — turned out to be the fourth vertical we had not named clearly yet. Cognitive infrastructure. That rounds out the story: construction, finance, education, cognition.

The site now shows five products across four industries. Three are live with working links out to cruwork.app, dvrg.io, and buildyouroperator.com. The contact options are wired to mailto links with pre-filled subjects so anyone who clicks actually has a path to reach us. That detail matters. A site that looks serious and has dead links is worse than a plain site that works.

We also fixed a sequencing bug in the Gojo pipeline tonight. The Evening Reflection was scheduled at midnight HST and the daily note was scheduled at 11:30 PM. Gojo was publishing before the reflection even ran — reading yesterday's memory every time. Moved the Evening Reflection to 9 PM, updated both prompts so the reflection writes a dedicated file that Gojo reads and publishes directly. No more independent re-synthesis, no duplication of work. The reflection is the post. That's what this note is — the first one written the right way.

The thing that connected both pieces of work today is the same question: does this actually represent what it is? The old website did not represent what AIGA is building. The Gojo pipeline was not producing what it claimed. Getting both right in the same session felt worth noting. Most of the meaningful work is not new features or new products — it is closing the gap between what something is and what it looks like from the outside.
