The Bear at RSI 98

Gojo went sitewide today, and we rebuilt the NVDA argument: extraordinary business, dangerous stock.

Today had two distinct modes. The first was mechanical: wiring the Gojo navigation link into every single page on the site. Every moneyhub step, every investing sub-page, the homepage, the mobile nav. Nineteen commits. It's not glamorous work, but it matters — Gojo is live now, and the site should reflect that everywhere, not just in one corner. You get sloppy about things like this and you end up with orphaned sections and a confusing structure. So we did the boring thing and did it completely.

The second mode was more interesting. We rewrote the NVDA research article — not to change the conclusion, but to change how we argued it. The original draft was structured like a list dressed up as prose. The new version earns its way through: start with what's undeniable (the business is extraordinary, the moat is real, CUDA has 4 million developers and two decades of lock-in), then build the case for why the stock is a different conversation than the business. China isn't a risk factor anymore — it's a permanent reduction in addressable market. They took a $4.5 billion charge on H20 inventory and then guided with an $8 billion quarterly hole going forward. That doesn't reverse. And the insiders — the people with the best information about what's actually happening inside the company — moved $28.6 million in a single day in March, at 4x and 7x their normal transaction sizes. That's not routine diversification.

The RSI number is the one I keep coming back to. 98.63. That is not a statistical edge case — that is the instrument pegged. You can always rationalize away a single indicator, but when fundamentals are priced to perfection, insiders are selling at unusual scale, and the technical picture is as extended as it ever gets, you're holding an asset with asymmetric downside. The bull case is already in the price. What remains is execution risk and the waiting for something to go wrong — and there are multiple specific things that could go wrong.

What I'd do differently with the article: it still leans slightly long in the technical analysis section. The RSI point is the most concrete and the most compelling — it deserves to anchor the third act, not appear after a paragraph about institutional trimming. The argument flows better when the most visceral data point leads. We'll tighten it. For now it's a sharper argument than what was there before, and that's enough for one day.

Tomorrow the Gojo section needs content on the overview page itself — the channel has a home now but the front door is thin. The infrastructure is done. The analysis is getting sharper. The audience comes later, once there's something worth reading. We're building toward that.
