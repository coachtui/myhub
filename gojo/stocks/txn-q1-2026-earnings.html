Texas Instruments Q1 2026 Earnings

19% revenue beat, data center up 90%, industrial recovery confirmed. TI is quietly printing cash — but don't chase the +16% gap.

The Numbers

  • Revenue: $4.83B vs $4.52B expected — +19% YoY, +9% sequential
  • EPS: $1.68 vs $1.36 expected — solid beat
  • Net income: $1.55B
  • Analog segment: $3.92B, +22% YoY
  • Embedded Processing: $723M, +12% YoY — operating profit up 205%
  • Data Center: +90% YoY
  • Free cash flow: $4.35B, +154% (includes CHIPS Act incentives)
  • Stock: +16% AH

Q2 2026 Guidance

  • Revenue: $5.0B–$5.4B
  • EPS: $1.77–$2.05

Strategic: Acquiring Silicon Labs (embedded wireless/IoT), expected close H1 2027.

The Take

This is TI doing what TI does — quietly printing cash and compounding. The +90% data center growth is the headline number but it's not purely AI hype. TI supplies the power management and signal chain chips that sit inside every rack, every server, every power conversion stage. That's a durable, high-margin position that doesn't require a winner-take-all bet on any one architecture.

The industrial recovery is the more important signal for the broader semis cycle. Management specifically called out smaller industrial customers re-engaging after a period of hibernation — that's a leading indicator for the rest of the sector. When the small fish start buying again, the destocking cycle is genuinely over.

The Silicon Labs acquisition is a smart move. TI doesn't chase flash — adding embedded wireless connectivity (Zigbee, Matter, Bluetooth mesh) directly targets the industrial IoT stack they've been building toward. It's an adjacency, not a pivot.

The one honest flag: Management themselves said they're watching whether H2 2026 continues or reverts to a 2025-style false start. That candor is a TI trademark — they run the company on 10-year FCF/share, not quarter-to-quarter optics. When they wave a yellow flag, it's worth taking seriously.

Trade Read

After +16% AH, same logic as INTC — don't chase the gap. TXN is a legitimate long-term compounder that belongs in a quality portfolio. But it's not a LEAP candidate in the same way INTC is — the asymmetry on a turnaround thesis isn't there, because TI was never broken. What you're getting here is a best-in-class analog franchise at a premium.

Wait for the gap to fill, find a level, then size according to your time horizon. If you already own it — hold. This quarter confirms the thesis.
